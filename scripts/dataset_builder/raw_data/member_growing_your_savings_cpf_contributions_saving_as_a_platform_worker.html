Growing your savings
Saving as a platform worker with CPF contributions

As a platform worker, your CPF contributions will be made through your platform operator on a monthly basis. This applies for all platform workers.

Updates on 1 Nov 2024:

For platform workers born before 1 January 1995, you may opt in to increase your CPF contributions here.

Definition
Who is a platform worker?

You are a platform worker (PW) if you

 

  • provide ride-hail or delivery services under a platform work agreement with a Platform Operator (PO), and receive a payment or benefit; and
  • are under the management control of the PO when providing the platform service
Changes at a glance:
CPF Contributions for Platform Workers
false
For all platform workers 

 

Your platform operator will deduct your CPF contributions as and when you earn and submit them to CPF Board every month. 

For platform workers born on 1 Jan 1995 or later
  • CPF contribution rates for you and platform operators will be gradually increased to match that for employees and employers. 
  • CPF contributions will go to your Ordinary, Special or Retirement, and MediSave Accounts, based on these allocation rates. 
  • You will see an increase in total earnings after factoring in additional CPF contributions from platform operators. 
  • Your platform operator will deduct your share of CPF contributions as and when you earn, and submit it to CPFB monthly, together with the platform operator share of CPF contributions. 
  • The government will support eligible lower-income platform workers with Platform Workers CPF Transition Support (PCTS) to minimise impact on take-home pay.

For platform workers born before 1 Jan 1995

If you opt in to increased CPF contributions: 

  • You will contribute at the same rates, and enjoy the same benefits, as platform workers born on 1 Jan 1995 or later.  
  • You will receive CPF contributions from your platform operator. 
  • Your platform operator will deduct your share of CPF contributions as and when you earn, and submit it to CPFB monthly, together with the platform operator share of CPF contributions. 
  • CPF contributions will go to your Ordinary, Special or Retirement, and MediSave Accounts, based on these allocation rates.

 

If you do not opt in to increased CPF contributions:

  • You will continue to contribute to your MediSave Account only. 
  • You will not receive CPF contributions from your platform operator. 
  • Only MediSave contributions will be deducted by your platform operator as and when you earn, and submitted to CPFB monthly.

How much to contribute
Calculating your CPF contributions
1) Determine your gross earnings

Your CPF contribution will be computed on a monthly basis. All payments related to your platform work will attract CPF contributions. You can view your gross earnings in the earnings slip provided by your platform operators. 

/service/article/what-are-the-platform-fee-components-included-in-gross-earnings-that-attract-cpf-contributions.html false
Learn more about payments that attract CPF contributions
2) Compute your net earnings using the Fixed Expense Deduction Amount

Your CPF contribution will be computed based on gross earnings minus the Fixed Expense Deduction Amount (FEDA). The FEDA varies according to the mode of transportation you use for the job.

 

Apply the respective FEDA to your gross earnings to compute your net earnings. 

Mode of Transport

FEDA

Cars, vans, lorries, trucks

60%

Motorcycles, power-assisted bicycles, motorised personal mobility devices

35%

Bicycles, on foot (including use of public transport)

 20%

Calculate your net earnings using the FEDA:

Net Earnings = Gross Earnings – Fixed Expense Deduction Amount (FEDA)

Learn more about Fixed Expense Deduction Amount
3) Compute your CPF contributions

Your CPF contribution rate is based on your net earnings, age group and opt-in status. 

CPF contributions = Net Earnings * Applicable CPF Contribution rate

CPF contribution rates are lower for older platform workers and those with monthly net earnings below $750.

CPF contributions are computed separately for each platform operator you work with. You do not need to make any CPF contributions on net earnings exceeding $102,000 in a year per platform operator.

Verify your CPF
How to verify my CPF contributions are correct?

Do verify your CPF contributions every month so that any errors can be rectified as early as possible

1

Look out for the CPF Contribution Alert

 

Get notified when your CPF contributions are credited in your CPF accounts. You are encouraged to keep your contact details updated to receive the alert.

 

Do check if your CPF contributions are promptly made.

false
2

Check your net earnings and CPF contributions.

 

Your platform operator is required to provide an itemised earnings statement, which includes the deduction of your share of CPF contributions. 

 

You can verify the net earnings and CPF paid against your earnings statement (CPF contribution is based on net earnings per month, you should refer to all earnings statement issued by your platform operator for the relevant month). 

 

Use the Platform Worker CPF Contribution calculator to verify your CPF contributions. 

/member/tools-and-services/calculators/platform-worker-cpf-contribution-calculator.html false
Go to Platform Worker CPF Contribution Calculator
3

Approach your platform operator if any discrepancies are found.

 

If your CPF contributions are not paid on time or discrepancies are found, please contact your platform operator promptly for clarification. Your platform operator is expected to rectify errors without delay. If your platform operator does not provide you with a satisfactory response, you can lodge a report with CPF Board.

 

For CPF contributions paid in error, your platform operator will need to submit a request for adjustment or refund immediately, and no later than one year from the date of payment.

Other income
If you are earning other income
Self - employed income

If you derive self-employed income from non-platform work (e.g. tuition teaching, or taxi street-hail jobs), or from platform work prior to 2025, you will still need to contribute to your MediSave account on these sources of income in the following year, after you have declared your net trade income to IRAS.

Employee income

If you are also earning employee income, your employer is still required to make contributions on your employee income to CPF Board every month.

Platform Workers CPF Transition Support
How can the Platform Workers CPF Transition Support help?

The Platform Workers CPF Transition Support (PCTS) will be provided from 2025 to 2028 as the CPF contribution rates for platform workers are gradually increased to align with employees.

 

PCTS will provide monthly cash support to offset part of the year-on-year increase in contribution rates to platform workers’ CPF Ordinary and Special or Retirement Account. 

Find out about Platform Workers CPF Transition Support
Workfare Income Supplement
Workfare Income Supplement for Platform Workers

Eligible lower-income platform workers will receive Workfare Income Supplement (WIS) payments monthly instead of annually from 2025. 

 

Eligible platform workers who are mandated or opt in to the increased CPF contribution rates will receive higher WIS payments at the same level as employees from 2029, once their CPF contribution rates fully align with that of employees. They will also receive a higher proportion of WIS payments in cash - 40% compared to 10% today.     

Find out about Workfare Income Supplement for platform workers
