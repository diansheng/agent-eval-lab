Growing your savings
CPF interest rates

(1 July 2026 to 30 September 2026)

Overview
Interest on your CPF savings

To help boost retirement savings, the Government pays extra interest on the first $60,000 of your combined balances, which is capped at $20,000 for Ordinary Account (OA). 

The extra interest earned on your OA savings will go into your Special Account (SA) or Retirement Account (RA) to enhance your retirement savings. 

If you’re participating in the CPF LIFE scheme, you will still earn the extra interest on your combined CPF balances, including savings used for CPF LIFE.

How are CPF interest rates determined?
More about CPF interest rates
Ordinary Account interest rate
Interest rate from 1 July 2026 to 30 September 2026: 2.5% per annum

Reviewed quarterly, this rate is computed based on the 3-month average of major local banks' interest rates1 (subject to the legislated minimum interest of 2.5% per annum).

 

1 0.32% for the period from February 2026 to April 2026.

Special, MediSave and Retirement Account interest rates
Interest rate from 1 July 2026 to 30 September 2026: 4% per annum

Reviewed quarterly, this rate is computed based on the 12-month average yield of 10-year Singapore Government Securities (10YSGS) plus 1%2 (subject to the current floor interest rate of 4% per annum3).

 

2 3.09% for the period from May 2025 to April 2026.

 

3 To help CPF members grow their savings, the Government will further extend the 4% floor rate for interest earned on all Special, MediSave and Retirement savings until 31 December 2026.

How are CPF savings invested?

Your CPF savings are invested in Special Singapore Government Securities (SSGS) which are guaranteed by the Government. SSGS are non-tradable bonds issued specifically to the CPF Board for the investment of CPF savings.

This ensures that CPF savings are safe, regardless of financial market conditions, and ensures that CPF Board will be able to pay the respective interest rates to you.
