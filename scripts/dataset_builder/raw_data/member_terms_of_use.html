Receipts​

  1. Once a payment transaction is completed, you will receive an acknowledgement transaction code​. You should retain this code as a reference for any subsequent communication with the Board as regards this transaction. The acknowledgement transaction code is not an acknowledgement that payment has been successfully received and processed by the Board.

  2. Receipts will only be issued to you if payment is subsequently received and processed by the Board successfully. If any payment is not cleared by the relevant bank, you would get an unsuccessful message from the relevant bank's website.

  3. For employers and platform operators who have made Direct Debit/GIRO arrangement with the Board, your bank account will not be debited immediately after you submitted your payment advice to the Board online. The Board will send debit instruction to your designated bank to debit your designated bank account for the amount submitted by you and on the date agreed by you.

  4. The time frame within which you must make good the payment depends on the nature of payment, e.g., the due date for CPF contributions is on the last day of the calendar month. You are expected to submit your CPF contributions for your employees and platform workers by the due date. Enforcement action would be taken against employers and platform operators who fail to pay by the 14th of the following month (or the next working day if the 14th falls on a Saturday, Sunday or Public Holiday). This includes imposing late payment interest charged at 1.5% per month commencing from the first day after the due date.

  5. Other than payment for CPF contributions, you can also make electronic payments for transactions types specified in our Website.

​​​​Refunds​

  1. Contributions paid in excess and refunds of excess contributions are governed by CPF laws. To apply for refund, you must complete the Board's refund application and submit this for the Board's consideration. The Board reserves the right to reject any refund application subject to CPF laws.​

  2. Once the Board has approved a refund application, the amount to be refunded will be credited back to the bank account stated in the refund application form.

  3. Other excess payments will be refunded by cheques, and depending on the nature of payment, the payor's written request may be required.

​Roll​backs

  1. If you made online submission a​nd your submission failed, our system will not send the information to backend for processing. Nevertheless, the Board will apply the following rollback actions:

    1. If the Board has not been credited and waiting for deduction, we will not submit file to the bank for deduction, we will delete deduction;

    2. If file has been submitted to the bank, we will need to retransfer the new file; and

    3. If payment has been credited to the Board, you will need to apply for refund.
