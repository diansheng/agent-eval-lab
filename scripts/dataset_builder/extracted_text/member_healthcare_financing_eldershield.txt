About
What is ElderShield
ElderShield is a long-term care insurance scheme targeted at severe disability, especially during old age.
Overview
When it was introduced in 2002, ElderShield provided payouts of $300/month for up to five years upon severe disability. It was subsequently reviewed in 2007 to provide better benefits of $400/month for up to six years. The benefits you will receive depends on which scheme you joined. Please note that the ElderShield scheme is no longer open for new applications.

Monthly payouts
ElderShield provides monthly cash payouts for up to 72 months to help pay out-of-pocket expenses in the event of  severe disability.

Fixed premiums
Premiums are determined when you enter the scheme and remain fixed. You pay them each year until the policy anniversary after turning age 65, or when you make a successful claim.

Government administration of ElderShield
From 1 November 2021, the Government has taken over the administration of ElderShield from private insurers (Singapore Life Ltd, Great Eastern Life Assurance Co Ltd, and Income Insurance Ltd).

Eligibility
Who’s covered under ElderShield
Overview
Who’s enrolled
Until 2019, all Singapore Citizens and Permanent Residents with MediSave Accounts were enrolled in ElderShield at the age of 40, unless they opted out.
Since 2020, auto-enrolments into ElderShield were discontinued. Singapore Citizens and Permanent Residents who turned 40 in 2020 were instead enrolled into CareShield Life.
If you were born on 30 September 1932 or before, or had a pre-existing disability as of 30 September 2002, you wouldn’t have been able to join ElderShield in 2002. Instead, you may be eligible for assistance under the Interim Disability Assistance Programme for the Elderly (IDAPE) if you develop severe disability.

What protection does ElderShield provide
Lifetime coverage
Continue to be covered for life once you’ve completed paying all your premiums at age 65.

Up to 72 months’ of cash payouts
Receive a monthly cash payout for up to five or six years, depending on the plan. With ElderShield 400, you’ll get $400 per month for up to 72 months, and with ElderShield 300, you’ll get $300 per month for up to 60 months.

Fully payable with MediSave
You can use your MediSave to pay your ElderShield premiums. Family members can help you by paying from their own MediSave, or topping up your account with cash.

Claims
When are you eligible to claim
You can apply to receive ElderShield payouts if you’re determined by an MOH-accredited assessor to be unable to carry out three of the six activities of daily living (ADL) listed below.
Washing
The ability to wash in the bath or shower (including getting into and out of the bath or shower) or wash by other means.
false
Dressing
The ability to put on, take off, secure, and unfasten all garments and, as appropriate, any braces, artificial limbs, or other surgical or medical appliances.
false
Feeding
The ability to feed oneself food after it has been prepared and made available.
false
Toileting
The ability to use the toilet or manage bowel and bladder function through the use of protective undergarments, such as diapers or surgical appliances, if appropriate.
false
Walking or moving around
The ability to move indoors from room to room on level surfaces.
false
Transferring
The ability to move from a bed to an upright chair or wheelchair, and vice versa.

Are there any fees involved?
The disability assessment fee is $100 for a clinic assessment and $250 for a house call, both of which are payable to the assessor at the time of assessment. The full fee will be reimbursed with the first payout if you are assessed to have developed severe disability.
